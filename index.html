01 — About

Who I am

I'm a graduate student at the University of Southern California pursuing an MS in Electrical Engineering with a focus on computer architecture, the discipline of designing the hardware that runs the world's most demanding software.

My work sits at the intersection of microarchitecture design, memory systems, and performance analysis. I've built branch predictors in C++ that intercept 11M+ live branches, simulated full cache hierarchies in gem5, and optimized out-of-order cores under tight area budgets.

I care about the why behind the silicon: understanding the trade-offs, not just the results. Before USC, I completed my B.E. in Electrical Engineering at College of Engineering Pune, where I built embedded systems and worked on EV test platforms at Bajaj Auto.
