01 — About

Who I am

I'm a graduate student at the University of Southern California pursuing an MS in Electrical Engineering, specializing in computer architecture, the discipline of designing the hardware that runs the world's most demanding software.

My work sits at the intersection of microarchitecture design, memory systems, and performance analysis. I have built branch predictors in C++ that intercept 11M+ live branches, simulated full cache hierarchies in gem5, and optimized out-of-order cores under tight area budgets.

I care about the why behind the silicon: understanding trade-offs, not just results. Before USC, I completed my B.E. in Electrical Engineering at College of Engineering Pune, where I built embedded systems and developed EV charger test platforms.

02 — Projects

Selected work

Project 01

Branch Prediction Architecture Design & Evaluation

Architected six branch predictors as Intel PIN instrumentation callbacks in C++, intercepting outcomes across 11M+ dynamic branches during live execution. Achieved 96% prediction accuracy with TAGE through geometric history length tuning, a 22% improvement over the static baseline.

96% prediction accuracy
C++ Intel PIN TAGE Microarchitecture
Project 02

Memory Hierarchy Design & Cache System Simulation

Evaluated L1 through L3 cache hierarchy depth on x86 in gem5, analyzing LRU, pseudo-LRU, and FIFO replacement policies across SPEC benchmarks. Identified configurations that minimized effective memory access latency and improved subsystem performance by 35%.

35% performance improvement
gem5 x86 SPEC Cache Design
Project 03

Processor Microarchitecture Design & Optimization

Increased IPC by 42% on a RISC-V out-of-order core in gem5 under a 300M transistor and 30mm² area budget, sweeping pipeline width, issue queue depth, and functional unit allocation to reach the Pareto-optimal design point.

42% IPC gain
RISC-V gem5 OOO Core Pareto Optimization
